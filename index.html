Overview

Exoplanets are planets outside our Solar System. Since the first confirmed discovery in 1992, thousands have been found, revealing incredible diversity in sizes, temperatures, and compositions.

Detection Methods

Transit Method

Scientists detect small dips in a starâ€™s brightness when a planet passes in front of it.

Radial Velocity

Measures the wobble of a star caused by the gravitational pull of an orbiting planet.

Direct Imaging

Rare technique where telescopes directly photograph large distant planets.

Gravitational Microlensing

Gravity bends and magnifies light, revealing planets passing in front of distant stars.

Types of Exoplanets

Scientists have found hot Jupiters, super-Earths, mini-Neptunes, and Earth-sized planets in habitable zones.

Important Missions

Kepler Space Telescope

Discovered thousands of exoplanets using the transit method.

TESS

TESS continues the search for exoplanets around bright nearby stars.

JWST

JWST studies exoplanet atmospheres for molecules like water vapor and methane.
