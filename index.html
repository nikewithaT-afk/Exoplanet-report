Investigating Exoplanets

Welcome! Explore detection methods, types of exoplanets, missions, and more!

Detection Methods

Transit Method: Detects dips in starlight when a planet passes in front of a star.
Radial Velocity: Measures a star's wobble due to gravitational pull of orbiting planets.
Direct Imaging: Rare direct photos of young, large exoplanets.
Gravitational Microlensing: Uses gravity to magnify distant stars, revealing planets.

Types of Exoplanets

Hot Jupiters: Gas giants very close to their stars.
Super-Earths: Larger than Earth, smaller than Neptune.
Mini-Neptunes: Small gas planets, larger than Earth but smaller than Neptune.
Earth-size planets: Rocky planets, some in habitable zones.

Important Missions

Kepler: Discovered thousands of exoplanets using transit method.
TESS: Continues to discover exoplanets with transit observations.
James Webb Telescope: Studying exoplanet atmospheres.
